Am I ready?

I've been boxing for 10 years, but I don't think I am ready to go pro.

It's been ten years since I first set my sights on boxing. When I was 12, I told my dad, “I want to become a professional boxer and a champion one day.” Fast forward five years; my days were filled with early mornings and constant training. I'd wake up at 7 a.m., focused mainly on boxing. By 8:30, I was at the gym, ready for my long workout routine

      Stretching
      jump rope
      jumping jacks
      shawdow boxing
      heavy bag work
      head movement drills
      pad work with dad
      speed bag
      abs
      cardio

This was my daily grind for five years, shaping my impressive amateur record of 116 wins and only 2 losses. But now, the real challenge lay ahead.

At 20, I was offered my first professional contract. The excitement was there, but so were the doubts. My opponent was undefeated at 5-0, and I wondered if I was truly ready. Many of my boxing friends went pro at 18 and became champions by 25. I aimed to achieve that same feat.

But when the contract came, I knew it wasn't the right time for me. I denied, choosing instead to dedicate the next year to unparalleled training. I was determined to refine my skills, and next year, I would seize that contract and make my mark in the boxing world.
